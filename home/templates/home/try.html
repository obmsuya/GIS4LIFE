Uses of GIS system.

GIS can be used as tool in both problem solving and decision making processes, as well as for visualization of data. GIS uses geographic information to help people answer questions and make decisions. GIS analyze trends, find relationships, and assist with future decisions making. Just like internet, GIS is for all proffessionals, GIS answers the following questions;

Map location

We can map the spatial location of real-world features and visualize the spatial relationships among them. Example, the map can illustrates the location of countries, rivers, and cities within the continent of Africa. Locational maps can be used to determine where things are situated in relation to each other and are often used with navigation .

Map quantities

People map quantities, such as where the most and least are, to find places that meet their criteria or to see the relationships between places. Example: The map can shows the cemetery locations as dots (dot density) and each district is color coded to show where the most and least are.

Map what is inside

We can use GIS to determine what is happening or what features are located inside a specific area/region. We can determine the characteristics of "inside" by creating specific criteria to define an area of interest (AOI). The map can illustrates school locations in relation to crime areas. Mapping what is inside can assist with monitoring and assessment.

Map densities

Sometimes it is more important to map concentrations, or a quantity normalized by area or total number. The map can illustrates a higher population density in the region. Density maps can be used to condense large amounts of data analyzed for correlations and trends. Further analysis could be completed to determine if the denser population is related to the proximity of the border or the proximity of water bodies.

What is nearby.

We can find out what is happening within a set distance of a feature or event by mapping what is nearby using geoprocessing tools like BUFFER. Mapping what is nearby is often used in risk assessment and predictions.

Map change

We can map the change in a specific geographic area to anticipate future conditions, decide on a course of action, or to evaluate the results of an action or policy. The map can illustrates population change over the past 10 years. and show where the the largest population changes occurred. This can be used to assess future trends in urban sprawl and market demands.
